This guide is coming soon. We are writing the definitive Anmeldung (address registration) guide for expats arriving in Germany. It will include step-by-step instructions, document checklists, appointment booking tips, and everything you need to navigate the Bürgeramt.

Legal requirement: You must register your address within 14 days of moving into your apartment. Failure to do so can result in a fine of up to €1,000.

What this guide will cover

The Anmeldung is the single most important bureaucratic step when arriving in Germany. It unlocks everything else: your Tax ID, bank accounts at traditional banks, health insurance enrollment, and more. Without it, you're effectively invisible to the German system. Our guide will walk you through the entire process, from booking an appointment to what happens after registration.

Documents you'll need (preview)

Passport or national ID card, completed Anmeldeformular (registration form — available at the Bürgeramt or online), Wohnungsgeberbestätigung (landlord confirmation — legally required since 2015), and your rental contract. Some Bürgerämter also ask for your marriage certificate if applicable.
