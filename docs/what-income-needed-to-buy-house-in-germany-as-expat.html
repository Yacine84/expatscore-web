What is actually happening

In Germany, the process of buying a house as an expat involves several steps, including obtaining a German bank account and a SCHUFA score. A good SCHUFA score is crucial in securing a mortgage, and expats can improve their score by maintaining a stable income and making timely payments on their blocked account Germany. The required income and savings vary depending on the location, with cities like Berlin and Munich requiring higher incomes due to the higher cost of living.

Why this happens to expats in Germany

Expats in Germany often face challenges when trying to buy a house due to the stringent requirements set by German banks. One of the primary reasons is the need for a stable income, which can be difficult for expats to demonstrate, especially if they are self-employed or have a variable income. Additionally, the requirement for a significant amount of savings can be a barrier for many expats, who may not have had the opportunity to build up their savings in Germany.

Step-by-step: how to fix it

To increase their chances of buying a house in Germany, expats can take several steps. Firstly, they should open a German bank account and apply for a blocked account Germany to demonstrate their financial stability. Secondly, they should work on improving their SCHUFA score by making timely payments and maintaining a stable income. Expats can also consider seeking the advice of a financial advisor who is experienced in helping expats navigate the German housing market.

What to do if you're stuck

If expats are struggling to meet the income and savings requirements, they can consider alternative options such as seeking a co-signer or exploring different types of mortgages. Frequently Asked Questions

What is the average income required to buy a house in Germany?
The average income required to buy a house in Germany varies depending on the location, but expats can expect to need an income of at least €50,000-€70,000 per year. Additionally, expats will need to have a significant amount of savings, typically 10-20% of the purchase price.

How can I improve my SCHUFA score as an expat in Germany?
Expats can improve their SCHUFA score by maintaining a stable income, making timely payments on their debts, and keeping their credit utilization low. It's also essential to monitor their credit report and dispute any errors or inaccuracies.

What are the benefits of having a blocked account Germany?
A blocked account Germany provides a secure and stable way for expats to demonstrate their financial stability and commitment to buying a house in Germany. It also helps expats to build a positive credit history and improve their chances of securing a mortgage.
