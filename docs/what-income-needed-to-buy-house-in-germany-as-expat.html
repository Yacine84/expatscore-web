Frequently Asked Questions

What is the average income needed to buy a house in Germany?
The average income needed to buy a house in Germany varies depending on the location, but as a general rule, expats should have a stable income of at least €50,000 per year. Additionally, they should have sufficient savings for a down payment, which is typically 20% of the purchase price.

How can I improve my SCHUFA score as an expat in Germany?
Expats can improve their SCHUFA score by making timely payments on their German bank account and credit cards. They can also consider opening a blocked account to demonstrate their financial stability. Furthermore, expats should avoid missing payments and keep their credit utilization ratio low to maintain a good credit history.

What are the requirements for a mortgage application in Germany?
The requirements for a mortgage application in Germany typically include a good SCHUFA score, a stable income, and sufficient savings for a down payment. Expats may also need to open a German bank account and provide documentation of their income and employment status. Additionally, they may need to provide a copy of their residence permit and proof of health insurance.
