What is actually happening

In Germany, the process of buying a house as an expat involves several steps, including obtaining a mortgage from a German bank. To do this, you'll need to have a good SCHUFA score, which is a credit score that lenders use to assess your creditworthiness. A good SCHUFA score requires a stable income, a German bank account, and a history of responsible financial behavior. The amount of income needed to buy a house in Germany as an expat varies depending on factors such as the location, size, and price of the property. However, as a general rule, lenders in Germany require borrowers to have a minimum income of around €50,000-€70,000 per year to qualify for a mortgage. Additionally, expats will need to have a significant amount of savings to cover the costs of buying a house in Germany, including the purchase price, notary fees, and other expenses.

Why this happens to expats in Germany

The reason why expats in Germany struggle to determine what income and savings are needed to buy a house is due to the complex and often confusing nature of the German banking system and bureaucratic requirements. For example, expats may not be familiar with the concept of a blocked account in Germany, which is a type of bank account that is required for international students and expats to demonstrate their financial resources. Furthermore, expats may not understand how to navigate the SCHUFA system, which can make it difficult to obtain a good credit score and qualify for a mortgage. To overcome these challenges, expats can seek the advice of a financial advisor or a mortgage broker who is familiar with the German banking system and can provide guidance on what income and savings are needed to buy a house in Germany.

Step-by-step: how to fix it

To determine what income and savings are needed to buy a house in Germany as an expat, follow these steps: First, research the cost of living in Germany and the price of properties in your desired location. This will give you an idea of how much income you'll need to support yourself and your mortgage payments. Second, check your SCHUFA score and work on improving it if necessary. This can involve paying off debts, building a credit history, and demonstrating responsible financial behavior. Third, open a German bank account and consider opening a blocked account in Germany to demonstrate your financial resources. Finally, consult with a financial advisor or mortgage broker to get a better understanding of what income and savings are needed to buy a house in Germany and to get pre-approved for a mortgage.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the minimum income required to buy a house in Germany as an expat?
The minimum income required to buy a house in Germany as an expat varies depending on factors such as the location, size, and price of the property. However, as a general rule, lenders in Germany require borrowers to have a minimum income of around €50,000-€70,000 per year to qualify for a mortgage.

How can I improve my SCHUFA score as an expat in Germany?
To improve your SCHUFA score as an expat in Germany, make sure to pay your bills on time, avoid taking on too much debt, and demonstrate responsible financial behavior. You can also consider opening a German bank account and making regular payments to build a credit history.

What is a blocked account in Germany and how do I open one?
A blocked account in Germany is a type of bank account that is required for international students and expats to demonstrate their financial resources. To open a blocked account in Germany, you'll need to contact a German bank and provide proof of your financial resources, such as a bank statement or a letter from your employer.
