GKV vs PKV: Which System Applies to You?

Germany operates a dual health insurance system. Before you even research TK, you need to know which half of the system you belong to — because the rules are different and the choice, once made, is not always reversible.

GKV (Gesetzliche Krankenversicherung — statutory health insurance) is mandatory for all employees earning below €77,400 per year in 2026. Contributions are income-based, shared with your employer, and capped. Children and non-working spouses are covered for free under family insurance (Familienversicherung). TK is a GKV insurer.

PKV (Private Krankenversicherung — private health insurance) is available to employees earning above €77,400/year, civil servants, and the self-employed. Contributions are based on age, health status, and coverage level — not income. Each family member pays separately. PKV can be significantly cheaper for young, healthy, high earners; it becomes expensive with age or health issues.

🎯 The Decision Rule for Expats

If your annual gross salary is below €77,400 in 2026, you are compulsorily insured with GKV. You cannot choose PKV. TK is one of your options within GKV. If you earn above this threshold, you have a choice — but switching from PKV back to GKV later is extremely difficult. Make this decision carefully, ideally with advice from an independent insurance broker.

Additionally, Pflegeversicherung (long-term care insurance) is mandatory and separate from health insurance. For most employees in 2026 the total Pflegeversicherung rate is 3.6%, split equally (1.8% you, 1.8% employer). Childless employees over 23 pay a 0.6% surcharge — bringing their share to 2.4%. Combined, your total social contribution for health + long-term care is approximately 10.5% of gross, employer-matched.

⚠ The Rate That Changed Dramatically
TK's Zusatzbeitrag doubled from 1.2% (2024) to 2.69% (2026)

In 2024, TK's supplementary rate was a market-leading 1.2%. It jumped to 2.45% in 2025, then 2.69% in 2026. This is the steepest increase among major German public insurers. What TK Covers — and What It Doesn't

All GKV insurers cover the same statutory benefits. TK adds specific extras on top. Here is the honest map — including the coverage gaps most expats discover too late.

🏥
Doctor Visits & Hospital

All GPs and specialists (Fachärzte) nationwide. Hospital treatment fully covered. No referral needed for most specialists. No deductible.

✦ TK Extra: English-speaking doctor finder (enter postcode + language)
💊
Prescription Medications

Covered with a co-payment of €5–€10 per prescription (maximum €10 per package). Annual co-pay cap: 2% of gross income (1% for chronically ill).

🧠
Mental Health & Therapy

Psychotherapy covered — but wait times for GKV-approved therapists are 3–6 months. TK's online programme (Instahelp) offers faster digital access.

✦ TK Extra: TK-Stressfrei app — mindfulness & stress courses, reimbursable
🦷
Dental Care

Basic dental — check-ups, fillings, extractions. Crowns/bridges: 60% covered after 5 years of consistent check-ups. Cosmetic and implants: not covered.

⚠ Important: Consider a private Zahnzusatzversicherung supplement
🤰
Maternity & Childbirth

All prenatal check-ups, midwife services, hospital birth, postnatal care. Fully covered. Elective C-section without medical indication: costs may apply.

✦ TK Extra: Baby-Lotse — personal midwife advisor available in English
🏃
Preventive Care & Screenings

Annual check-ups (Vorsorge) from age 35, cancer screenings per STIKO schedule, vaccinations, occupational health assessments.

✦ TK Extra: Bonus points for preventive activities → cash back (see below)
🧘
Alternative Medicine

Osteopathy: up to 3 sessions/year, €40 per session. Certified Heilpraktiker (alternative practitioners): partially reimbursable via Bonus Program.

✈️
Emergency Care Abroad

EHIC (EU Health Insurance Card): covers medical emergencies in all EU/EEA countries. Outside EU: emergency repatriation and some emergency treatment covered. Not a travel insurance substitute.

⚠ Coverage Gap Most Expats Miss
Glasses & Contact Lenses: almost never covered

The most common complaint in expat forums. TK only covers glasses or contact lenses for severely visually impaired members (legal blindness threshold). Standard prescription glasses — even strong prescriptions — are not covered. If you wear glasses or contacts, budget €150–€400/year out-of-pocket, or add a private vision supplement.

The TK Bonus Program: Free Cash Most Members Never Claim

This is the most under-used benefit in TK. The Bonusprogramm rewards preventive health behaviour with direct cash payments — but you must register for it manually, and you must actively submit your activities. TK does not remind you. Most expats discover it years after joining, if at all.

💶 Typical Annual Value: €50–€150 cash back

Activities earn points. Points convert to euros paid directly to your bank account once per year. The programme is completely free and available to all TK members. Register at Meine TK — even if you cannot navigate the German interface, the Bonusprogramm section is findable with browser translation.

📋 How to Activate the Bonus Program

Log into meine.tk.de → select "Bonusprogramm" in the sidebar → click "Jetzt anmelden" (register now). Browser-translate to English. Once registered, activities that TK processes automatically (like GP billing) are tracked without further action. For manual activities (courses, osteopathy), upload receipts via the TK app or web portal.

How to Enroll in TK — Step by Step

Enroll before your employment start date — not after. If you miss this, your employer assigns a Krankenkasse on your behalf.

01

Apply at tk.de/en or via the Feather service (English-guided)

Direct TK: fast, free, fully in English via their international portal. Feather: also in English, slightly more guided, offers additional support. Both produce the same membership. Apply at least 5–7 business days before your first day of work.

02

Upload your documents

You need: passport or national ID. Optionally: your German address (needed for card delivery). If you don't yet have an address, you can complete enrollment and update the address later — but your insurance card won't ship until an address is on file.

03

Receive your Mitgliedsbescheinigung (1–2 business days)

This is your membership confirmation — the document HR needs. Send it to your employer's HR/payroll immediately. This is what activates the correct tax and insurance deductions on your payslip. Do not wait for the physical health card.

04

Receive your Gesundheitskarte (electronic health card) by post

Your eGK arrives within 1–2 weeks. Until it arrives, doctors accept your Mitgliedsbescheinigung as proof of insurance. You can visit a GP the day after enrolling — you don't need the physical card.

05

Register for the Bonus Program in Meine TK

This step is not automatic. Log in to meine.tk.de, navigate to Bonusprogramm, and register. Set a reminder to submit your first preventive check-up receipt. Most members leave €75–€150/year unclaimed because they forget this step.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does TK health insurance cost in 2026?▼
TK's total contribution rate is 17.29% of gross income — 14.6% statutory + 2.69% TK Zusatzbeitrag. As an employee, your employer pays exactly half (8.645%), so you pay 8.645% from your payslip. On a €3,500/month salary, your contribution is approximately €302/month. Contributions are capped at €5,812.50/month gross — meaning the maximum monthly employee contribution is around €503. Use the calculator above for your exact figure.
Is TK better than AOK for expats?▼
For most expats, yes. TK has the lowest rate among major insurers (2.69% Zusatzbeitrag vs ~2.85% AOK average), English-language app and support in 6 languages, and a 4.3/5 Trustpilot rating. AOK is a regional federation — rates and service quality vary significantly by Bundesland. If you are in Bavaria, AOK Bayern is worth comparing, as it sometimes has competitive rates and strong regional presence. Otherwise, TK wins on English support, digital services, and consistency.
Can I join TK before starting my job in Germany?▼
Yes — and you should. Apply at least one week before your employment start date. TK will issue your Mitgliedsbescheinigung within 1–2 business days. Give this document to HR before your first payroll run. If you miss the deadline, your employer legally assigns you to a Krankenkasse — and you may not have chosen that one.
What is the TK Bonus Program and how much can I earn?▼
The TK Bonusprogramm pays cash for preventive health activities — check-ups, dental visits, vaccinations, certified health courses, and osteopathy sessions. Most active members earn €50–€150/year, paid directly to their bank account. You must register manually in Meine TK — it is not automatic. Most expats never claim it.
What does TK not cover?▼
The main gaps: (1) Glasses and contact lenses — only covered for near-legally-blind members. (2) Dental crowns, bridges, implants — only partially covered after 5 years of consistent check-ups; cosmetic and implants are not covered. (3) Psychotherapy — covered but 3–6 month waiting times for GKV-approved therapists. (4) Homeopathy — TK historically offered it but coverage has been reduced. For dental gaps, consider a private Zahnzusatzversicherung supplement.
Is TK valid for a German visa or residence permit?▼
Yes. TK membership is accepted as proof of health insurance for all German visa categories — employment visa, Blue Card, student visa, freelancer visa. TK issues a Versicherungsbescheinigung (insurance confirmation letter) in German and English upon request, free of charge, within 1–2 business days. This is the document embassies and Ausländerbehörden require.
How does TK handle health insurance for my family in Germany?▼
If your spouse is not working (or earns below €505/month in 2026), they can be added to your TK policy for free under Familienversicherung. Children are covered for free until 18 (23 if in education). This is one of GKV's major advantages over PKV, where each family member pays a separate premium. To add a family member, submit their passport and a completed Familienversicherung form at any TK branch or online.
