What is actually happening

When you move to Germany, you're required to pay the ARD ZDF Beitrag, a monthly fee of €18.36 for public broadcasting services. This fee is typically paid through a German bank account, and if you don't deregister, you'll continue to be charged even after leaving the country. To make matters worse, if you have a blocked account in Germany, you may not even be aware that the fees are being deducted, as the account is not actively being used.

Why this happens to expats in Germany

Many expats in Germany are not aware of the need to deregister from the ARD ZDF Beitrag service when leaving the country. This can be due to a lack of information or the complexity of the German bureaucracy. As a result, expats may receive unexpected bills or even have their SCHUFA score affected, making it more difficult to obtain credit or loans in the future. It's crucial to understand that deregistering from the ARD ZDF Beitrag is a separate process from closing a German bank account or de-registering with the local authorities.

Step-by-step: how to fix it

To deregister from the ARD ZDF Beitrag, you'll need to fill out a form on the ARD ZDF website or contact their customer service directly. You'll need to provide your registration number, address, and the date you're leaving Germany. It's recommended to do this at least two weeks before your departure to ensure that the deregistration is processed in time. Additionally, make sure to close any German bank accounts and de-register with the local authorities to avoid any further complications.

What to do if you're stuck

If you've already left Germany and are still receiving bills from the ARD ZDF Beitrag, don't panic. You can still deregister by contacting the ARD ZDF customer service and providing proof of your departure, such as a flight ticket or a residence permit from your new country. If you're having trouble with the deregistration process, you can also consider seeking help from an expat community or a reputable relocation service in Germany. Remember to also check your SCHUFA score to ensure that it hasn't been affected by any unpaid ARD ZDF Beitrag fees.

Quick tip: Act now and deregister from the ARD ZDF Beitrag service at least two weeks before leaving Germany to avoid any unwanted bills or complications with your SCHUFA score. You can find the deregistration form on the ARD ZDF website or contact their customer service directly for assistance.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to deregister from the ARD ZDF Beitrag if I'm only leaving Germany temporarily?
Yes, it's recommended to deregister from the ARD ZDF Beitrag even if you're only leaving Germany temporarily, as you'll still be charged the monthly fee if you don't. You can always re-register when you return to Germany.

Can I deregister from the ARD ZDF Beitrag online?
Yes, you can deregister from the ARD ZDF Beitrag online through their website. You'll need to fill out a form and provide your registration number, address, and the date you're leaving Germany.

Will deregistering from the ARD ZDF Beitrag affect my SCHUFA score?
No, deregistering from the ARD ZDF Beitrag will not directly affect your SCHUFA score. However, if you have any unpaid ARD ZDF Beitrag fees, it may negatively impact your credit score.
