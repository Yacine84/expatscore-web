What is actually happening

The German government requires expats to complete an integration course to demonstrate their commitment to German society. This course covers language skills, cultural orientation, and civic education. The course typically lasts 12-18 months and includes a final exam. The BAMF (Federal Office for Migration and Refugees) oversees the integration course program, ensuring that expats meet the necessary German permanent residence integration course requirements. To increase your chances of a successful application, focus on achieving a high score in the integration course, which can also positively impact your SCHUFA score.

Why this happens to expats in Germany

Expats often underestimate the importance of the integration course or struggle to balance it with work and other responsibilities. Additionally, language barriers and limited knowledge of the German bureaucracy can lead to missed deadlines or incomplete applications. To avoid these pitfalls, expats should open a German bank account, such as a blocked account in Germany, to demonstrate financial stability and facilitate communication with the authorities. By understanding the German permanent residence integration course requirements and taking proactive steps, expats can reduce the risk of application rejection.

Step-by-step: how to fix it

To overcome a rejected permanent residence application due to incomplete German permanent residence integration course requirements, expats should first contact the local Ausländerbehörde (foreigners' registration office) to clarify the reasons for the rejection. Next, they should enroll in an integration course and focus on achieving a high score. Expats can also consider seeking guidance from the ExpatScore.de community or consulting with a relocation expert to navigate the process. By taking these steps, expats can demonstrate their commitment to integrating into German society and increase their chances of a successful permanent residence application.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the integration course?
The integration course is a program designed for expats to learn about German language, culture, and society, and to demonstrate their commitment to integrating into German society. The course typically lasts 12-18 months and includes a final exam.

How does the integration course impact my SCHUFA score?
Completing the integration course can positively impact your SCHUFA score, as it demonstrates your ability to integrate into German society and manage your financial responsibilities. A good SCHUFA score can also help you when opening a German bank account or applying for a loan.

Can I complete the integration course online?
While some course providers offer online components, the integration course typically requires in-person attendance. Expats should check with their local course provider to determine the specific requirements and course format.
