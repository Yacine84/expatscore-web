What is a German Blocked Account (Sperrkonto)?

A Sperrkonto (blocked account) is a mandatory financial instrument for non-EU international students and job seekers applying for a German student or job-seeker visa. It proves to German authorities that you can financially support yourself during your stay — without relying on state assistance.

The account is "blocked" in the sense that you cannot withdraw the full amount at once. Once you arrive in Germany and activate the account, €992 is released monthly. You need a separate regular bank account (Girokonto) to receive these monthly transfers.

The 2026 required amount of €11,904 is calculated based on Germany's BAföG (Federal Training Assistance Act) living costs estimate for students. This amount is reviewed annually and has increased steadily: €853/month in 2020, €934/month in 2023, €992/month in 2025/2026.

⚠ Coracle Update (August 2025): Coracle has suspended new blocked account applications as of August 2025. We recommend Expatrio or Fintiba for new applications. Existing Coracle accounts continue to function normally.

Step-by-Step: How to Open a Blocked Account

The complete process from choosing a provider to activation in Germany takes approximately 2–4 weeks. Start at least 6–8 weeks before your visa appointment to leave buffer for delays.

1
Choose Your Provider
For most students: Expatrio (lowest fees, fastest, US citizens accepted, includes Girokonto). For students under 18 or needing DAK insurance: Fintiba.
Time required: 1 day
2
Create Account & Upload Documents
Register online, upload your passport (exactly as it appears — full name, middle names, correct spelling). Expatrio requires only passport. Fintiba may request additional enrollment documents.
Time required: 1–2 hours
3
Transfer €11,904 + Buffer
Transfer exactly €11,904 from your home bank. Add €100–200 buffer for transfer fees. Important: Use only approved transfer methods (SEPA, SWIFT via provider instructions). The name on transfer must exactly match your passport. Note: Kotak Mahindra (Indian bank) may not be accepted — check with your embassy.
Time required: 5–10 business days for international transfer
4
Receive Sperrbestätigung (Blocking Confirmation)
Once your transfer is verified, the provider issues an official Blocking Confirmation letter. This is the critical document for your visa appointment. Print on quality paper — bring original + 2 copies to your embassy interview.
Time required: 3–7 days after funds clear
5
Visa Interview
Present the Sperrbestätigung as part of your financial proof package. All German embassies worldwide accept Expatrio and Fintiba confirmation letters. Keep the document secure — without it, the visa application cannot proceed.
Within 2 weeks of receiving confirmation (typical)
6
Arrive in Germany & Activate Account
Within your first 14 days: complete Anmeldung (address registration at Bürgeramt). Then: send your Aufenthaltstitel (residence permit) copy to your provider online. Monthly €992 payments begin immediately after activation to your German Girokonto.
Must activate within first 90 days of arrival

Most Common Mistakes That Cause Visa Rejection

  • This is the #1 cause of rejection. The name on your blocked account must match your passport exactly — including all middle names, hyphens, and special characters. Even a single missing middle name can lead to automatic rejection. Triple-check before submitting.
  • International bank transfers can take 5–10 business days. Provider verification takes another 3–7 days. Apply 6–8 weeks before your visa appointment. Many students apply 2 weeks before — and miss the confirmation letter deadline.
  • Germany only accepts European partner banks for the escrow model. Blocked account providers must work with banks holding a German banking license (for direct banking) or partner with EU financial service providers. If your local bank is used directly, it will not be accepted. Always use Expatrio, Fintiba, or Coracle — all partner with BaFin-regulated German banks.
  • The full €11,904 must be deposited and confirmed before the Sperrbestätigung is issued. Partial transfers are not accepted. If your country has currency controls or bank transfer limits per day, plan accordingly — it may take multiple days of transfers.
