The cost of getting a German driver's license as an expat can range from €2,000 to €5,000 or more, depending on the type of license and the number of lessons required. This is because expats often need to take additional theory and practical lessons to meet the German driving standards. Furthermore, the cost of a German driver's license includes fees for the license itself, as well as costs associated with opening a German bank account, which is often required for payment. Why this happens to expats in Germany

The high cost of getting a German driver's license as an expat is due in part to the strict regulations and requirements set by the German government. Expats must meet the same standards as German citizens, which can be challenging, especially for those who are not familiar with the German traffic laws. Additionally, the cost of living in Germany, including the cost of a German bank account and other expat-related expenses, can add to the overall expense. As an expat in Germany, it's essential to understand the process and find ways to reduce costs, such as by taking advantage of online resources and discounts offered by driving schools.

Step-by-step: how to fix it

To save money on a German driver's license as an expat, start by researching driving schools that offer discounts for expats or bundle deals that include theory and practical lessons. You can also consider taking online theory lessons to reduce the number of in-person lessons required. Additionally, shop around for the best deal on a German bank account, and look for options that offer a free or low-cost blocked account. By taking these steps, you can reduce the overall cost of getting a German driver's license and make the process more affordable.
