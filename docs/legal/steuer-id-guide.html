What is the Steuer-ID?

The Steueridentifikationsnummer — almost universally called the Steuer-ID — is your permanent, 11-digit personal tax identification number in Germany. It is issued by the Bundeszentralamt für Steuern (Federal Central Tax Office, BZSt) and assigned to every person who registers an address in Germany, regardless of nationality or citizenship status.

Unlike most administrative numbers in Germany, the Steuer-ID has one exceptional property: it never changes. You receive it once. You keep it for life, even if you move, change jobs, get married, become a freelancer, or temporarily leave Germany. It is the single constant in the otherwise labyrinthine German tax system.

🔑 The Steuer-ID is the Master Key to Germany

Without your Steuer-ID, your employer defaults to the maximum tax rate (Class 6) — costing you hundreds of euros per month. Your bank cannot complete CRS/FATCA reporting. Your SCHUFA score-building is delayed. Your Kindergeld application is impossible. This one 11-digit number unlocks your entire German financial life. Get it on day one.

The 4 German Tax Numbers Decoded

The most common mistake every expat makes: using the wrong number in the wrong context. Germany has four distinct tax identification numbers. They are not interchangeable. Here is the complete decoder:

Everyone
Steuer-ID
12 345 678 901 · 11 digits · permanent

Your personal tax identification number. Assigned automatically after Anmeldung. Never changes. Required by every employer, every bank, and for every tax return.

Who needs it: All employees, freelancers, students, retirees — anyone registered in Germany.
Freelancers
Steuernummer
12/345/67890 · 10–13 digits · changes with address

Your business tax number, issued by your local Finanzamt when you register as self-employed. Changes when you move to a different Finanzamt district. Goes on all your invoices to German clients.

Who needs it: Freelancers, self-employed, and businesses in Germany. Employees do not need this.
EU Business
Umsatzsteuer-ID
DE 123 456 789 · 9 digits · starts with DE

German VAT number. Required for B2B transactions within the EU. You apply for it via Elster when you register your business. Not needed if your annual turnover is below €25,000 (Kleinunternehmerregelung).

Who needs it: Businesses with EU clients. Freelancers over the €25,000 threshold or with EU clients.
New 2024
Unternehmens­basisnummer
DE-[Steuer-ID]-based · new standard

A new unified business identifier introduced in 2024 to replace the Steuernummer for B2B purposes. Based on your Steuer-ID. Most businesses will receive theirs automatically — no application needed. Still being rolled out.

Who needs it: Registered businesses in Germany. Most relevance is administrative — not yet required for invoicing.
⚠ The Classic Mistake
Giving your employer the Steuernummer instead of the Steuer-ID

They look similar. One is 11 digits (Steuer-ID: "12 345 678 901"), the other is 10–13 digits with slashes (Steuernummer: "12/345/67890"). Payroll systems will reject the wrong number, which triggers Class 6 taxing. Always give your employer the plain 11-digit number — no slashes, no spaces required for the system, though ELSEAM uses the format with spaces.

How to Get Your Steuer-ID in 2026

There are three routes. Which one you use depends on your timeline.

A

Standard Route: Complete Anmeldung → wait for the letter (2–6 weeks)

Register your address at the Bürgeramt. The BZSt automatically generates your Steuer-ID and mails it to your registered address. No further action required. This is the normal path for anyone who has time. Make sure your name is on your mailbox. Letters returned undelivered mean a 6+ week delay to reissue.

B

Fast-Track: Visit your local Finanzamt same-day

If your Anmeldung is complete but you haven't received the letter, walk into any Finanzamt with your passport and Meldebescheinigung (the registration confirmation from the Bürgeramt). A staff member can retrieve your Steuer-ID from the system and provide it verbally or in writing. No appointment needed. You can do this the day after your Anmeldung — the Finanzamt's system updates within 24–48 hours of your registration being processed.

C

Online Request: BZSt re-issue form (4–6 weeks, post only)

If you have already been in Germany previously and had a Steuer-ID that you lost, use the official BZSt re-issue form at bzst.de. For security reasons, the BZSt only sends tax IDs by post to your registered address — they will never email or phone it to you. This route is the slowest. If someone claims to send you a Steuer-ID digitally, it is a scam.

The Anmeldung in 2026: Online vs In-Person

The Anmeldung is the address registration that triggers everything — your Steuer-ID, your SCHUFA stability record, your bank account access, your Kindergeld eligibility. Since late 2024, the process has fundamentally changed in major cities.

💻
Online Anmeldung via BundID
✦ New 2024–2026

Available in Berlin, Munich, Hamburg, Frankfurt, Cologne, and growing. You register through the BundID digital identity portal — no appointment, no queue. Requires a German eID-enabled passport or a BundID account verified via Post-Ident.

You need
  • BundID account or eID-capable passport
  • Wohnungsgeberbestätigung (landlord confirmation) — digital copy accepted
  • Your German address (post your Anmeldung to yourself to confirm)
🏛️
In-Person at Bürgeramt
Traditional Route

Still required in smaller cities and towns. Book an appointment online (termine.berlin.de, muenchen.de/termin, etc.) — waits of 2–8 weeks are normal. Some offices offer walk-in slots early morning. Bring all documents in paper form.

You need
  • Passport or national ID (original)
  • Wohnungsgeberbestätigung — signed original from landlord
  • Completed Anmeldeformular (the registration form — download from city website)
  • If married: spouse's passport too
⏰ The 14-Day Rule — Critical

German law requires you to register your address within 14 days of moving in. There is no criminal penalty for being late, but you may be fined up to €1,000 in some Bundesländer for significant delays. More practically: every day without your Anmeldung is a day without your Steuer-ID — which costs you money at work and delays your SCHUFA history. Prioritise this on arrival day.

The 6 German Tax Classes: What They Mean for Your Salary

Germany has six income tax classes (Steuerklassen). Your class is assigned automatically based on your situation. It directly determines how much income tax is deducted from every payslip. Understanding yours is not optional — getting it wrong costs real money every month.

Class Who it applies to Key Allowances 2026 Expat Context
I Single, divorced, widowed (not Class II) — the default for most new expat employees Basic allowance: €11,784 · Pension lump sum: €1,230 · Work lump sum: €1,230 Most expats start here Reasonable rate. Can be changed if circumstances change.
II Single parents with at least one child in the household All Class I allowances + Relief for single parents: €4,260 Apply at Finanzamt if this applies. Significantly lower deductions.
III Married/registered partner, used by the higher earner (paired with Class V for partner) Double the basic allowance: €23,568 · Favourable rate Best class for the higher earner in a couple. Requires Class V for the other partner.
IV Married/registered partners with similar income (equal split) Same as Class I allowances for each partner Fairer than III/V if both partners earn similar amounts. Avoids surprise bills in tax return.
V Married/registered partner, used by the lower earner (paired with Class III) No basic allowance — very high deductions Very high deductions. Only beneficial as a couple if the difference in income is large.
VI Second job, or anyone who has not provided their Steuer-ID to employer No allowances whatsoever — maximum possible deduction Avoid at all costs Temporary for expats awaiting their Steuer-ID. All overpaid tax recoverable via Steuererklärung.

Freelancers & Self-Employed: Your Full Tax Number Roadmap

If you are employed, stop reading here — your Steuer-ID is all you need. If you are freelancing in Germany or plan to, you need to understand the additional steps. They run in sequence and cannot be skipped.

01

Get your Steuer-ID first (via Anmeldung)

This is always the starting point. Everything else depends on it. Cannot be bypassed.

02

Submit the Fragebogen zur steuerlichen Erfassung (via Elster)

The dreaded 7-page German form that registers you as a freelancer with the Finanzamt. Available at elster.de. Only available in German. Use the Sorted app (free, English-guided version) to fill it out without a Steuerberater. Submit it within one month of starting freelance work.

03

Receive your Steuernummer (4–8 weeks)

Your local Finanzamt issues your Steuernummer by post. Format: "12/345/67890" (format varies by Bundesland). This goes on all invoices to German clients. It changes if you move Finanzamt districts. Cannot be used for EU clients — for those you need the Umsatzsteuer-ID.

04

Apply for Umsatzsteuer-ID if you have EU clients (optional)

If your annual turnover exceeds €25,000 or you have B2B clients in other EU countries, you need a German VAT number (Umsatzsteuer-ID, format: DE123456789). Apply via Elster. Issued in 2–4 weeks. Below €25,000, you may operate under Kleinunternehmerregelung (no VAT charged, simpler accounting).

05

File quarterly Umsatzsteuervoranmeldungen if VAT-registered

Once VAT-registered, you must submit quarterly VAT pre-declarations via Elster. This is non-negotiable and has strict deadlines. A Steuerberater (tax advisor, €500–€1,500/year) is strongly recommended at this stage — the complexity justifies the cost.

Once you have your Steuer-ID — open your bank account next

N26 (BaFin-licensed, English app, SCHUFA-building) is the fastest account for new arrivals. Takes 15 minutes.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between Steuer-ID and Steuernummer?▼
The Steuer-ID (11 digits, e.g. "12 345 678 901") is permanent, assigned to you personally, and never changes. The Steuernummer (10–13 digits, e.g. "12/345/67890") is assigned to freelancers and businesses by the local Finanzamt and changes when you move districts. Employees only need the Steuer-ID. Freelancers need both.
How long does it take to receive the Steuer-ID after Anmeldung?▼
The BZSt sends your Steuer-ID letter within 2–6 weeks after your Anmeldung is processed. September through December are peak migration months — waits can extend to 8 weeks. Fast-track alternative: visit your Finanzamt with your passport and Meldebescheinigung. They can provide it same-day from their system.
Can I work without a Steuer-ID in Germany?▼
Yes — legally you can start work without it. But your employer must apply Tax Class 6, which has no allowances and deducts the maximum possible tax from your salary. On a €3,500 gross salary, this costs €400–€600/month in extra deductions. You recover the overpayment by filing your Steuererklärung (tax return), but you lose the cash flow in the meantime. Get your Steuer-ID before your first payroll if at all possible.
Is online Anmeldung possible in Germany in 2026?▼
Yes — in major cities. Since late 2024, Berlin, Munich, Hamburg, Frankfurt, and Cologne offer online registration via the BundID portal. You need a BundID account or a German eID-activated passport. In smaller cities and towns, in-person Bürgeramt registration is still required. Always check your specific city's website before assuming the online option is available.
Does the Steuer-ID affect my SCHUFA score?▼
Indirectly. The Steuer-ID itself is not reported to SCHUFA. However, the Anmeldung required to obtain it registers your stable German address — which is a positive SCHUFA stability signal. Banks that request your Tax ID (like N26, DKB) are BaFin-licensed and report to SCHUFA. So the chain is: Anmeldung → Steuer-ID → bank account → SCHUFA history. See our complete SCHUFA Guide for the full picture.
Can I get my Steuer-ID before completing the Anmeldung?▼
No — the Anmeldung is the trigger that generates your Steuer-ID. There is no workaround. Some banks (like N26) will open an account before you have the Steuer-ID and ask for it later, but the Tax ID itself cannot exist without a registered German address. Complete the Anmeldung on arrival — within 14 days of moving into your accommodation.
What happens to my Steuer-ID if I leave Germany?▼
Your Steuer-ID remains valid and unchanged forever. It is not deactivated when you de-register (Abmeldung) or leave Germany. When you return to Germany — even years later — you use the same Steuer-ID. Keep a record of it before leaving. If you lose it, you can request a re-issue via the BZSt form, but only if you have a valid registered address in Germany at the time of the request.
