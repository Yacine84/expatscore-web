Let's confirm your IT specialist status
Germany's Blue Card has a special pathway for experienced tech professionals without a university degree. Answer three quick questions.
Do you have at least 3 years of professional IT experience within the last 7 years?
Is your job offer in the IT or technology sector in Germany?
Can you provide proof of your IT skills — work references, portfolio, certificates, or verifiable employment history?
⚠ The IT pathway requires all three conditions
  • Without proof of experience or an IT role, the Federal Employment Agency will not approve your application.
  • Consider building your documentation (references, LinkedIn, GitHub, certifications) before applying.
🗺️
The Blue Card is not your route — but Germany has alternatives
The EU Blue Card specifically requires either a university degree or documented IT expertise. Your profile doesn't match these criteria — but that doesn't mean Germany is closed to you. Here are the pathways that may apply.
🛠️ Skilled Worker Visa (§ 18a / 18b AufenthG)
For vocational/trade qualifications. Requires recognition of your qualification in Germany. No minimum salary — but your qualification must be officially recognised.
🌟 Opportunity Card (Chancenkarte)
A 1-year job-seeker visa introduced in 2024. Points-based. Find a job in Germany first, then convert to a work visa. Good starting point without a fixed offer.
📋 Already in Germany? Get your Steuer-ID first
If you're already working in Germany on any permit, your Tax ID is the first administrative step. Our guide covers it in full.
